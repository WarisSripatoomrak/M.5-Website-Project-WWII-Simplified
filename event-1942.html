The Battle of Midway (4/6/1942 - 7/6/1942)

Battle of Midway.
(National Archives, Washington, D.C.)
    1. A naval battle between Japan and United States at Midway Atoll
    2. The allies had the advantage as the United States won the battle.
    3. The United States and Japan participated in a tremendous naval combat during World War II. Japanese naval forces attempted to take Midway Island by assaulting the numerically inferior US Pacific fleet. After deciphering the Japanese naval code, the US prepared for the attack by dispatching around 115 ground-based aircraft and three aircraft carriers. On June 3, its planes began assaulting Japan's carrier force. After suffering significant casualties, Japan abandoned preparations to land on Midway. The fight brought Japan's and the United States' Pacific naval forces close to parity and was a watershed moment in their conflict.
